About the Book

Black Skin, Bold Heart is the story of what happens when a Black woman rises to the top of Canadian healthcare leadership — and what it costs. It’s the story of what happens next, when she decides the cost is too high and walks away from the C-suite to build something on her own terms.

Over three decades, Aileen M. Edwards climbed from HR Director to Chief Human Resources Officer, navigating the unwritten rules of corporate leadership while managing the invisible weight of being the only one in the room. She led organizational transformations, managed labor relations crises, built leadership teams, and earned every credential the profession has to offer.

But alongside every achievement was an accumulation of micro-aggressions, code-switching, unspoken expectations, and the exhausting performance of belonging in spaces that were never designed for her presence.

“This book is about the messy middle — the space between making it and making sense of it. It’s for anyone who has ever wondered whether the seat at the table was worth the price of admission.”

Black Skin, Bold Heart is a memoir about executive identity, institutional betrayal, the myth of meritocracy, and the radical act of choosing alignment over status. It is written for leaders navigating similar terrain, for organizations trying to understand why their DEI efforts aren’t working, and for anyone who has ever felt the tension between who they are and who they were told to become.
