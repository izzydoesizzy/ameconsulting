About Aileen

Thirty years of executive leadership. One mission: to be the coach and guide she never had.

Credentials

  • ◆ Master of Arts in Leadership (MA)
  • ◆ Associate Certified Coach, ICF (ACC)
  • ◆ Registered Clinical Counsellor (RCC)
  • ◆ Board Certified Counsellor (BCACC)
  • ◆ Certified Crucial Conversations Facilitator
  • ◆ CliftonStrengths Certified Coach

Aileen M. Edwards is a leadership development consultant, executive coach, DEIA strategist, and author with more than 30 years of progressive leadership experience in Canadian healthcare.

Her career arc spans from HR Director to Chief Human Resources Officer at Markham Stouffville Hospital, to Senior Director of People, Culture and Organizational Development at Providence Healthcare (now part of Unity Health Toronto). Throughout, she led enterprise-level organizational development, labor relations, and culture transformation initiatives for multi-site hospital systems with thousands of employees.

In founding AME Consulting, Aileen made a deliberate choice: to leave the C-suite and build a practice dedicated to being the coach she never had. She brings the hard-won lessons of navigating corporate leadership as a Black woman — the micro-aggressions, the unwritten rules, the exhausting need to code-switch — into a coaching and consulting practice that doesn’t shy away from the real dynamics of power, identity, and belonging in the workplace.

“I dedicated my career to being the coach I never had — someone who could have helped me navigate the unwritten rules, who understood what it means to lead authentically while managing the politics of being the only one in the room.”

Her proprietary BOLD™ Method — Becoming, Owning, Leading, Disrupting — is the framework she uses across her coaching, leadership development programs, workshops, and keynotes. It’s built on a core insight from decades of executive experience: under pressure, leaders don’t rise to the moment — they fall back on who they’ve already practiced being.

The Work

Today, Aileen works with organizations across Ontario and beyond, delivering leadership development programs, DEIA consulting engagements, executive coaching, and independent workplace investigations. Her clients include healthcare systems, children’s services organizations, nonprofits, educational institutions, and corporate teams.

Her leadership development programs integrate CliftonStrengths mapping, 360 assessments, emotional intelligence workshops, and strategic behavioral sessions. Her workplace investigations under Ontario’s Bill 168 and the Human Rights Code produce reports that withstand arbitration scrutiny. Her DEIA work is grounded in lived experience, not theory.

Beyond the Practice

Aileen currently serves as Chair of the Board at Three Oaks Foundation, the primary domestic violence shelter for Hastings and Prince Edward Counties, and as a member of the Board of Directors at Quinte Health Care, a multi-site hospital system serving the Quinte region.

She is a member of the Weiss International coaching network, Black Leaders Worldwide, the Queen’s University WE-CAN initiative, and the International Coaching Federation. She was featured in Executives Diary Magazine for her work in leadership development and organizational transformation.

Her memoir, Black Skin, Bold Heart, chronicles 30 years of navigating corporate spaces not built for her — and the reckoning of choosing alignment over status.

Affiliations

Aileen lives in Ontario’s Quinte West region and is the mother of three. She works with clients virtually across Canada and in-person throughout Ontario.
