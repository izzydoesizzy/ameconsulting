Speaking & Keynotes

Aileen M. Edwards delivers keynotes and workshops that challenge audiences to rethink leadership, belonging, and the courage it takes to lead differently.

Inquire About Speaking Availability

Aileen M. Edwards, MA, ACC is a leadership development consultant, executive coach, and author of Black Skin, Bold Heart. With more than 30 years of executive leadership in Canadian healthcare, including roles as CHRO and Senior Director of People, Culture and Organizational Development, Aileen brings a rare combination of lived experience and professional expertise to every stage she occupies. Her talks are grounded in the proprietary BOLD™ Method and draw from her journey navigating corporate leadership as a Black woman — making them equal parts powerful, practical, and personal.

Signature Keynotes

1

“Stop Auditioning for Belonging”

Why inclusion can’t be earned and what organizations get wrong about belonging. A talk about the invisible labor of code-switching, the myth of meritocracy, and what it actually takes to build cultures where people don’t have to perform their way to a seat at the table.

2

“When the C-Suite No Longer Fits”

The reckoning of reaching the top and realizing it’s not where you belong. For audiences navigating career transitions, organizational misalignment, or the tension between achievement and authenticity.

3

“Under Pressure, You Don’t Rise — You Fall Back”

The core insight behind the BOLD™ Method. Under pressure, leaders don’t rise to the moment — they fall back on who they’ve already practiced being. A practical framework for building the leadership habits that hold when it matters most.

4

“Upholding Boundaries Is a Business Strategy”

Why boundaries aren’t a personal luxury — they’re a leadership practice. How setting and maintaining boundaries protects your energy, your team, and your organization’s culture.

5

“Black Skin, Bold Heart” — The Book Talk

A candid, powerful conversation drawn from Aileen’s memoir about race, identity, institutional betrayal, and the radical act of choosing alignment over status. Ideal for conferences, leadership retreats, and organizations committed to authentic DEI dialogue.
