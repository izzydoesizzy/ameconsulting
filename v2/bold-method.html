A Proprietary Framework

The BOLD™ Method

A coaching and leadership framework grounded in three decades of executive experience. Four pillars that transform how leaders show up under pressure.

B

Pillar One

Becoming

Shedding the stories that teach women to stay small. Before you can lead differently, you have to see yourself differently.

Becoming is about excavating the narratives you’ve internalized — about who gets to lead, who gets to speak, who gets to take up space. It’s the foundation everything else is built on.

In Practice

Identify and challenge limiting leadership narratives

Redefine what authority looks like on your own terms

Build a leadership identity rooted in authenticity, not performance

Pillar Two

Owning

Claiming authority and worth without apology. You’ve earned your seat. It’s time to stop justifying your presence in it.

Owning is about moving from permission-seeking to self-authorization. It’s about naming your value clearly and setting boundaries that protect your capacity to lead.

In Practice

Articulate your value proposition with clarity and confidence

Set and hold boundaries as a leadership practice

Navigate salary, scope, and status negotiations from a position of worth

O
L

Pillar Three

Leading

Influencing under pressure and building cultures of trust. Leadership isn’t a title — it’s a practice you return to every day.

Leading is about mastering the strategic conversations, the difficult decisions, the accountability that holds teams together when things get hard.

In Practice

Navigate high-stakes and crucial conversations with composure

Build accountability structures that outlast any single leader

Create psychologically safe team environments

Pillar Four

Disrupting

Redesigning what leadership looks like rather than accepting sameness. This is where the BOLD Method becomes a force for organizational change.

Disrupting is about refusing to replicate the systems that excluded you. It’s about building something different — leadership cultures where belonging isn’t something you audition for.

In Practice

Challenge organizational norms that no longer serve the mission

Design inclusive systems, not just inclusive training

Leave organizations better than you found them
